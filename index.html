EXHIBITION SPACE INFO

At some point, everything started looking the same, feeling the same, and meaning the same.

Your thoughts stopped being challenged.

You stopped wondering.

Invisible world

As adults, we lose sight of play.

The concept becomes almost alien to us, something we outgrew, or so we believe.

This exhibition is that hidden world of play, the one we stopped seeing.

It exists to remind us that play was never gone, only our perception changed.

If we let play back into our lives, we begin to see it everywhere,

woven into the ordinary, waiting in the everyday.

Surrealist Exhibition

Visitor Advisory

This exhibition contains surrealist imagery that some viewers may find unsettling, disorienting, or disturbing. Works may include themes of bodily distortion, dreamlike or nightmarish scenarios, psychological tension, and other confronting visual content. Parental discretion is advised for younger visitors. If you have any concerns, please contact us before your visit.

Omnipresent Optics

Step softly, for the floor is a stage and the audience is watching from within the masonry. Your optics bring to life the remnants of many playful souls- yearning for a spark of the antics they once possessed. but their stay is not permanent, and so you must continue their wishes- to relish in life's play.

Familiar Fluctuations

A stream of familiarity that has been stretched and punctured by the distortion of time. You look for a recognisable feature, a sister's smile, a father's brow, but the image refuses to settle into a single person. These are the true images of our embodiments, caught in a state of constant transit. Even here, in the distorted wave, the 'play' remains our constant companion, a vibrant thread streaming through our presence.

The Tessaract

The cube calls. Your dome becomes a chasm for the unknown- its cubic abyss fragmenting your mind and memories, expulsing them across a digital cosmos, your restlessness is stripped away, leaving only the pure, primal essence of play within. This oculus scours the mental clutter, purging the grey noise of the concrete jungle. What remains is a clarity, allowing you to finally perceive the world's true, hidden colours, the secret joy hidden behind the mundane.

Unspoken Observer

Behind these colossal, unblinking spheres lies a soul adrift in darkness, yet he harbors no malice. His pupils drag heavily with every stride you take, anchored to your movement by a desperate, unspoken truth: the play within you has not died; it is merely dormant. Though he yearns to scream this revelation, he has been denied a mouth, left only with the agonizing duty of observation. He watches, waiting for the moment you recognize the spark he sees so clearly. Will you spare him the pain of his silence and choose to see the play again?
